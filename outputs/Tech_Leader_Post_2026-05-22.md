🚀 Sundar Pichai: Google Declares the "Agentic Era" — Inside the Biggest I/O Keynote in Its History

🎥 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYSncx9zLIU

Google just held the most consequential I/O keynote it has ever staged — and the message was unmistakable: the AI assistant era is over. In a packed two-hour keynote at Shoreline Amphitheater, CEO Sundar Pichai laid out how Gemini is moving from answering your questions to actually running your digital life.

Here's what he actually said:

⸻

Key Takeaways

1️⃣ The Agentic Gemini Era Is Officially Here 🤖
"We're firmly in our agentic Gemini era." Pichai framed the entire keynote around a single shift — Gemini moving from a "smart chatbot" to a system that "acts in the background on your behalf," planning, reasoning, and completing multi-step tasks under supervision. It's the clearest signal yet that Google believes the prompt-and-response era of AI is already ending.

2️⃣ Gemini's Scale Is Now Staggering 📈
The Gemini app crossed 900 million monthly active users — more than double the 400 million it had a year ago — while daily requests grew sevenfold. Behind the scenes, Google's APIs now process 19 billion tokens every minute and over 3.2 quadrillion tokens a month, up from just 9.7 trillion two years ago. With 13 Google products now topping a billion users each, AI is no longer a feature — it's the substrate of everything Google ships.

3️⃣ Gemini 3.5 Flash Ships — Free, Today ⚡
Pichai announced Gemini 3.5 Flash is "available today for everyone," beating the previous 3.1 Pro model across nearly every benchmark with an especially huge leap in coding. The model is purpose-built for agentic coding, long-horizon tasks, and real-world workflows — not just chat. Putting frontier-class capability in a free, fast tier is a direct shot at every rival charging premium prices for less.

4️⃣ Gemini Omni: One Model, Any Modality 🎨
Google unveiled Gemini Omni, an "any-to-any" model that takes any input and generates output in any modality — text, image, audio, or video. It's already live inside the Gemini app, Google Flow, and YouTube Shorts. This collapses the old patchwork of separate models for each media type into a single unified system.

5️⃣ Gemini Intelligence Moves Into the Operating System 📱
The most structural announcement: Gemini Intelligence, an agentic layer baked directly into Android 17, ChromeOS, Wear OS, and Android Auto at the OS level. It observes device context, anticipates what you want, and completes multi-step actions across third-party apps on your behalf. This turns the phone itself — not an app — into the AI agent.

6️⃣ Search's "Deepest Change in History" 🔍
Pichai called the transformation of Search "the deepest change in the history of the product." AI Mode hit 1 billion users within twelve months of launch, queries now average three times longer than traditional searches, and Project Astra is built directly into it for live, camera-based, multimodal questions. Google is rebuilding its crown jewel around conversation instead of blue links.

7️⃣ Antigravity 2.0 Built a Working OS in 12 Hours 💻
Google's new agent-first desktop IDE, Antigravity 2.0, runs on Gemini 3.5 Flash with a claimed 12x improvement in token efficiency. In a live demo, engineer Varun Mohan showed Antigravity build a functioning operating system from scratch in just 12 hours — using 93 subagents, 15,000 model requests, and 2.6 billion tokens. It's a vivid glimpse of software that increasingly writes itself.

8️⃣ The $190 Billion Bet on AI 💰
Pichai confirmed Google expects to spend roughly $180–190 billion on capital expenditure this year — up from just $31 billion in 2022, a sixfold increase in four years. The spending funds the data centers, chips, and energy needed to serve quadrillions of tokens every month. It's one of the largest infrastructure bets in corporate history, and Google is signaling it has no intention of slowing down.

9️⃣ Android XR Glasses Are Finally Real 👓
Google showed its Android XR glasses in hands-on demos for the first time, in two flavors — an audio-only version and one with a transparent in-lens display for navigation, real-time translation, and notifications. The audio glasses ship this fall, designed with Warby Parker and Gentle Monster, built on Samsung hardware, and they pair with both Android and iOS. After years of false starts, Google's wearable AI ambitions finally have a shipping date.

🔟 Ask YouTube + a Push for AI Transparency 🎬
Google introduced "Ask YouTube," which lets you query videos conversationally and jump straight to the most relevant moment — testing now, broader this summer. On trust, Pichai announced that OpenAI, Kakao, and ElevenLabs are adopting Google's SynthID watermarking, and that Content Credentials verification is expanding across Search and Chrome to flag AI-generated content. As synthetic media floods the web, Google is positioning itself as the arbiter of what's real.

⸻

Final Thought

💡 Google I/O 2026 wasn't a list of features — it was a declaration that the assistant era is over and the agent era has begun. With 900 million users, a $190 billion war chest, and AI woven into the operating system itself, Google is no longer playing catch-up in the AI race — it's setting the pace. Whether you build software, run a business, or just use a phone, the way you interact with computers is about to change beneath your feet.
