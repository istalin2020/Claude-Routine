🚀 Jensen Huang: Agentic AI Is About to Rewire a $50 Trillion Economy — and Every SaaS Company Will Become "AaaS"

🎥 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AZ9ySZESED0

Live from ServiceNow Knowledge 2026 in Las Vegas, Nvidia's Jensen Huang and ServiceNow's Bill McDermott sat down with CNBC's Jon Fortt for what may be the most consequential enterprise-AI interview of the year. The headline: agents aren't a feature anymore — they're the new workforce, and the entire software industry is about to be rebuilt.

Here's what he actually said:

⸻

Key Takeaways

1️⃣ The $50 Trillion Rewrite 💰
"This is one of the greatest transformations for the software industry ever. For the first time, service is software. Software is service, and the service industry is 100x larger than the software industry." Huang's framing reframes the AI opportunity entirely — agents don't just sell into the ~$650B SaaS TAM, they unlock the ~$50 trillion global services economy that humans currently run. That's why he and McDermott keep calling this bigger than the cloud, the smartphone, or the PC combined.

2️⃣ Robots Managing Robots, End-to-End 🤖🏭
"The entire manufacturing line will be operated by robots, managed by more robots, and the entire factory is a robot. They're going to run on top of ServiceNow, and you manage them just like employees." Huang painted a near-future where physical and digital agents share one control plane, governed exactly like human staff — onboarded, audited, off-boarded. It's the clearest articulation yet of his "physical AI" thesis colliding with the enterprise stack.

3️⃣ Every SaaS Becomes AaaS — Agentic-as-a-Service ☁️
"Virtually all SaaS companies will become AaaS — Agentic as a Service." Huang directly rebutted the "SaaSpocalypse" narrative spooking software stocks, calling agentic AI "fully accretive" for software companies rather than a replacement threat. His logic: agents need governed workflows, identity, audit, and data — exactly what incumbents like ServiceNow, Salesforce, SAP, and Microsoft already own.

4️⃣ Human Agents and AI Agents — Same Governance 👥⚙️
"You have human agents and AI agents, and they basically govern the same way." Huang argued that the org chart of the future has both species of worker reporting into the same HRIS, ticketing, and compliance systems. That's why the new ServiceNow + NVIDIA collaboration extends RBAC, audit trails, and policy enforcement to AI workers — not as bolt-ons, but as first-class identities.

5️⃣ Compute for Agents Has Exploded 1,000% in Two Years ⚡
The compute required for agentic AI is up roughly 1,000% versus generative AI just two years ago — because agents don't just answer, they read, reason, plan, call tools, and emit far more tokens in real time. Huang framed this as the structural reason inference demand keeps blowing past every analyst forecast. Translation for investors: the "AI peaked" thesis is fighting an exponential it doesn't understand.

6️⃣ Nvidia's Own Receipts: Support Tickets Cut by Two-Thirds 📉
"Of course, we use ServiceNow to support all of our employees. We've cut the amount of interaction with any employee intervention, any employee support issue, by two-thirds." Huang offered a rare hard ROI number from inside Nvidia itself — not a hypothetical, but production data on a workforce that's adding thousands of engineers a year. It's the kind of internal proof point CIOs have been desperate for.

7️⃣ ServiceNow as the "AI Enterprise Operating System" 🧠
"I said two years ago that ServiceNow is essentially the AI enterprise operating system, and that is really coming true." Huang doubled down on a prediction that sounded bold in 2024 and looks prophetic in 2026, as ServiceNow rebrands itself the "AI Control Tower for business reinvention." The strategic implication: the real moat in enterprise AI isn't the model — it's the workflow, identity, and governance layer agents have to plug into.

8️⃣ Project Arc + NVIDIA OpenShell — The Sandbox for Autonomous Workers 🛡️
The two CEOs unveiled Project Arc, a self-evolving autonomous desktop agent that "thinks, writes code, executes, and adapts when things don't go as expected" across enterprise tools — running inside NVIDIA OpenShell, a new open-source secure runtime for sandboxed, policy-governed agents. Every file read, command executed, and API called is logged into ServiceNow's AI Control Tower. It's the first real answer to the "agents will go rogue" objection that has frozen most enterprise rollouts.

9️⃣ McDermott's Warning: "Governance Isn't a Feature, It's the Whole Ballgame" 🚨
"Governance isn't a feature, it's the whole ballgame. Because without it, your whole company could come down," McDermott said, citing that "cybercrime is the third-largest economy behind the United States and China — a trillion-dollar-a-month problem." He hammered that today most agents are deployed with "no identity, audit trail or compliance posture" — a blast radius CISOs cannot tolerate. Expect AI governance budgets to be the fastest-growing IT line item of 2026.

🔟 The 50-Million-Worker Shortage Agents Will Fill 🌍
"The world will face a labour shortage of up to 50 million workers by 2030. But at this exact moment in time, billions of agents and robots are coming online," McDermott said, reframing the AI-takes-jobs narrative as AI-fills-jobs-that-don't-exist. With Huang nodding along, the duo positioned agentic AI not as a layoff engine but as the only realistic answer to global demographic decline. It's the most optimistic — and arguably most defensible — case for AI we've heard from a Fortune 500 stage all year.

⸻

Final Thought

💡 This was a rare moment where the picks-and-shovels king (Huang) and the workflow king (McDermott) jointly declared the next chapter of enterprise computing — and they agree the unit of work is no longer the SaaS seat but the governed agent. If they're right, every CIO budget, every software P&L, and every job description gets rewritten over the next 36 months. Watch this clip before the next earnings call you take seriously.
