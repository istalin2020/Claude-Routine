🚀 Jensen Huang: Nvidia's Demand Just Went "Parabolic" — and a Brand-New $200B Market Is Already Here

🎥 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CxysykYyRG8

Nvidia just posted a record $81.6B quarter, and CEO Jensen Huang sat down with CNBC's Sara Eisen to unpack what comes next. From quietly conceding China to Huawei, to building AI data centers in orbit, this interview reframes just how massive the AI buildout has become.

Here's what he actually said:

⸻

Key Takeaways

1️⃣ Agentic AI Has Officially Arrived 🤖
"This was an extraordinary quarter. Demand has gone parabolic. The reason is simple: Agentic AI has arrived." Nvidia's revenue surged 85% year-over-year to $81.62 billion, with data-center revenue alone doubling to $75.2 billion — a staggering 92% of the entire company. The shift from chatbots to autonomous AI agents isn't a future trend anymore — it's already the demand engine driving record results.

2️⃣ A Brand-New $200 Billion Market 💰
"Vera opens a brand new $200 billion TAM for Nvidia — a market we have never addressed before, and every major hyperscaler is partnering with us to deploy it." Huang revealed Nvidia has already sold $20 billion of its new Vera CPU this year, calling it "the world's first CPU purpose-built for agentic AI." It's a bet that billions of AI agents will each need their own CPU-driven compute — opening a market Nvidia never touched before.

3️⃣ Nvidia Just Conceded China to Huawei 🇨🇳
"Huawei is very, very strong. They had a record year... their local ecosystem of chip companies is doing quite well, because we've evacuated that market." Huang admitted Nvidia has "largely conceded" China's AI chip market amid U.S. export restrictions. It's a stunning reversal for a company that once dominated there — and a warning that the global AI race is fracturing into two separate ecosystems.

4️⃣ Data Centers Are Going to Space 🛰️
"Space computing, the final frontier, has arrived. Intelligence must live wherever data is generated." Huang detailed Nvidia's Vera Rubin Space-1 module for orbital data centers, even flagging the wild engineering hurdle: "In space, there's no convection, there's just radiation" — meaning cooling has to be completely reinvented. AI infrastructure is now literally leaving the planet.

5️⃣ Nvidia Runs Every Frontier Model 🧠
"With Anthropic we are scaling very quickly — we have big plans for them." Huang said Nvidia is "the only platform that runs every frontier AI model," name-checking Anthropic, OpenAI, xAI, Meta and Google's Gemini. While rivals build chips locked to one model family, Nvidia positioned itself as the neutral ground the entire industry is forced to run on.

6️⃣ AI Is a "Five-Layer Cake" 🍰
Huang framed AI as a five-layer industrial stack: energy at the base, then chips, infrastructure, models, and applications on top. "Every successful application pulls on every layer beneath it, all the way down to the power plant that keeps it alive." It's how he justifies Nvidia investing across the entire stack — and why he calls it "the largest infrastructure buildout in human history."

7️⃣ "We Don't Have Twice of Anything" ⚡
"I don't think we have twice as much power as we need, I don't think we have twice as much chip supply as we need — I don't think we have twice of anything." Huang was unusually candid that every layer of the supply chain is a hard constraint right now. Yet he insisted Nvidia can still meet its growth: "Everything is challenging at the scale we are working on."

8️⃣ $80 Billion Buyback, 25x Dividend Hike 📈
Nvidia authorized a fresh $80 billion share repurchase and raised its quarterly dividend to 25 cents from just 1 cent. It also guided to $91 billion in revenue next quarter — well above the ~$87 billion Wall Street expected. This is a company aggressively returning cash to shareholders while still forecasting explosive growth ahead.

9️⃣ The Stock Slid Anyway — and Huang Pushed Back 🔮
Despite the blowout numbers, Nvidia's stock dipped, and Huang told Sara Eisen the "concerns around the company have been clearly addressed." He urged investors to stop fixating on quarterly jitters and look at the multi-year arc of the AI buildout instead. The disconnect between record results and a nervous market is the real story of this quarter.

⸻

Final Thought

💡 Nvidia's quarter wasn't just big — it redrew the map of where AI is heading. Huang is no longer just selling chips; he's selling an entire industrial system that stretches from power plants to orbit, and a future where billions of AI agents need compute everywhere. The real question isn't whether the AI buildout is real — it's whether the rest of the world can keep up with the pace Nvidia just set.
