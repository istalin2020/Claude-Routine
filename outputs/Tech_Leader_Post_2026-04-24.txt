Sam Altman and Greg Brockman sat down together for the FIRST TIME EVER on a media podcast — and held absolutely nothing back. From killing Sora to firing at Anthropic and daring Elon Musk to take them to court, this 90-minute bombshell on Core Memory with Ashlee Vance changes everything we thought we knew about OpenAI's direction.

Here's what they actually said:

Key Takeaways

1️⃣ Sora Is Dead — Every Byte of Compute Goes to "Spud" 🪦
"I felt terrible," Altman admitted, describing the call to Disney's CEO Josh D'Amaro telling him the news. OpenAI killed its flagship video AI Sora after it collapsed from 1M to under 500K users while burning $1 million per day. Every ounce of compute has been redirected to "Spud" (GPT-5.5), the mysterious new model that Altman calls "the last major milestone before AGI" — which launched just two days later on April 23.

2️⃣ Anthropic Is Running a "Fear-Based Marketing" Campaign ⚔️
"It is clearly incredible marketing to say: 'We have built a bomb,'" Altman said, taking direct aim at Anthropic's new cybersecurity model Mythos. He accused rival Dario Amodei of weaponizing fear to position Anthropic as "the trustworthy people" who deserve to control AI, calling the move strategic cynicism dressed up as safety concern. This is the sharpest public attack Altman has ever leveled at his former colleagues.

3️⃣ Altman WANTS His Day in Court With Elon ⚖️
Sam revealed he is actually worried Musk will drop the $134 billion lawsuit before the trial begins — a stunning admission that Altman is hungry for a public airing of the facts. The Musk v. Altman trial begins April 27 in Oakland, with Musk claiming OpenAI betrayed its nonprofit mission. OpenAI has dismissed the suit as a "harassment campaign driven by ego, jealousy, and a desire to slow down a competitor."

4️⃣ "Personal AGI" — Your AI Knows You Before You Do 🧠
Altman laid out OpenAI's next product frontier: a "personal AGI" that "knows your calendar and your taste and books the concert ticket without asking." This is AI that anticipates your needs rather than waiting for prompts — proactive, deeply personalized, and always on. OpenAI expects AI "research interns" operating at scale by end of 2026, with fully autonomous AI researchers arriving by 2028.

5️⃣ The First-Ever Sam & Greg Joint Interview in 10 Years 🤝
In ten years of OpenAI, Altman and Brockman had never done a media podcast together — until now. Greg revealed their operating dynamic with rare candor: "Sam keeps raising the target, and Greg keeps forcing focus back." The joint appearance sends a clear signal that OpenAI is entering a new era of transparency, even as lawsuits, investigations, and firebombs close in from every direction.

6️⃣ Disney Got Blindsided — Less Than One Hour's Notice 🎬
Disney signed a billion-dollar Sora character licensing deal in December 2025, only to learn the product was being killed less than an hour before the public announcement. Disney has since shelved plans to invest $1 billion in OpenAI entirely. Altman said the painful call was necessary to "concentrate our compute and product capacity into the next generation of automated researchers and companies."

7️⃣ GPT-5.5 "Spud": 96.4% MMLU, 40% Faster, 1M-Token Context 🚀
Two days after this podcast, OpenAI launched GPT-5.5 on April 23 — codenamed Spud. It benchmarks at 96.4% on MMLU, runs 40% faster than GPT-4o, and holds a one-million-token context window. Altman describes it as the engine for a planned "super app" that merges ChatGPT, Codex, and the Atlas browser into a single seamless interface — a fundamental shift in how people interact with AI.

8️⃣ The Restructuring: Why OpenAI Had to Break the Mission ♻️
Altman and Brockman gave their clearest defense yet of OpenAI's controversial 2025 shift from a pure nonprofit to a capped-profit entity with a 26% nonprofit stake. Without massive external capital, they argued, xAI and Google would have lapped them on compute before AGI was reached. The restructuring is also the central battleground in the Musk lawsuit starting this week — making this week the most consequential in OpenAI's history.

9️⃣ New Yorker Fallout: Firebombed at Home, Still Moving 🔥
Just days before this podcast, Ronan Farrow's New Yorker investigation — 100+ interviews, 200+ pages of secret internal memos — called Altman "unconstrained by truth," with multiple senior insiders using the word "sociopathic" unprompted. A senior Microsoft executive reportedly compared Altman to Bernie Madoff. His home was then firebombed with a Molotov cocktail by an anti-AI activist. On the podcast, Altman acknowledged being too "conflict-averse" — a tendency he says "has caused great pain for me and OpenAI."

🔟 "The Economy Could 10x" — AI's Civilization Moment Is Here 📈
Altman and Brockman closed with a sweeping, almost audacious vision: AI agents as full research interns by end of 2026, fully autonomous AI researchers by 2028, and productivity gains so large they could expand the global economy tenfold. "The way Anthropic talks about OpenAI doesn't help," Altman said pointedly — but made clear he believes the abundance era is coming regardless of the noise, the lawsuits, or the bombs.
