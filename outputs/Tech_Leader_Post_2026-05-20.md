🚀 Sundar Pichai: "We Are Firmly in Our Agentic Gemini Era" — Inside Google I/O 2026's Biggest AI Reveal Yet

🎥 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c3PzJWN1Df4

Google just took the wraps off its most aggressive AI lineup ever — and the message was unmistakable: the chatbot era is over, the agent era has begun. From a model that "simulates the world" to a personal agent that runs your digital life 24/7, here's what Sundar Pichai and Demis Hassabis put on stage in Mountain View.

Here's what he actually said:

⸻

Key Takeaways

1️⃣ The "Agentic Gemini Era" Is Officially Here 🤖
"We are firmly in our agentic Gemini era," Pichai declared before a packed Shoreline Amphitheatre. He framed the entire keynote around a shift from AI that responds to AI that acts — software that completes tasks autonomously instead of waiting for instructions. But he stayed candid: "it's still early days when it comes to making agents easy to use, super secure and truly helpful."

2️⃣ AI Usage Has Gone Vertical — 3.2 Quadrillion Tokens a Month ⚡
Pichai dropped a jaw-dropping stat to prove AI demand is exploding: Google now processes over 3.2 quadrillion tokens per month — up from 480 trillion last year and just 9.7 trillion two years ago. That's a 7x jump in twelve months, with 8.5 million developers building on Google's models and APIs handling 19 billion tokens every minute. It's one of the clearest signals yet that AI adoption isn't slowing down.

3️⃣ Gemini 3.5 Flash: Frontier Brains at Bargain Speed 🧠
Google made Gemini 3.5 Flash the new default across the Gemini app, Search's AI Mode, and the API — and the pitch is bold. It beats last generation's Gemini 3.1 Pro on coding, agentic, and multimodal benchmarks while running at 4x the output speed and roughly 25% lower cost. Gemini 3.5 Pro is already in internal testing and ships next month.

4️⃣ Gemini Spark: A Personal Agent That Never Sleeps 🔆
Google unveiled Gemini Spark, a personal AI agent that runs 24/7 in the cloud "so you don't need to keep your laptop open." Built on Gemini 3.5, Spark can watch your credit card statements for sneaky subscriptions, track emails from your kid's school, and pull project notes from Gmail into a finished Doc — all on your behalf. It rolls out to Google AI Ultra subscribers in beta next week.

5️⃣ Gemini Omni: An AI That "Simulates the World" 🌍
Demis Hassabis took the stage to introduce Gemini Omni, a model that "can create anything from any input" by fusing Gemini's intelligence with Google's generative media models. Calling it "a step towards artificial general intelligence," Hassabis said Google spent the past year turning Gemini into "a world model AI that can understand and simulate the world" — better at simulating gravity and kinetic energy. Omni Flash is available today in the Gemini app, Flow, and YouTube Shorts.

6️⃣ Search Gets Its Biggest Redesign Ever 🔎
Pichai announced a sweeping agentic overhaul of Google Search, the engine used by over 3 billion people. For long projects like planning a wedding or managing a move, Search now spins up coding agents that build custom "mini apps" and dashboards, plus background "information agents" that surface what you need at exactly the right moment. "As we enter this agentic era, Search will be more helpful and powerful than ever," he said.

7️⃣ Android XR Smart Glasses Are Finally Real 👓
Google and Samsung officially revealed Android XR smart glasses, shipping this fall at an expected $600–$900. Two tiers arrive — audio-only frames and an in-lens display version — with Gemini built in for real-time translation, navigation, and visual understanding, plus designer frames from Gentle Monster and Warby Parker. It's Google's most serious shot yet at Meta's Ray-Ban dominance.

8️⃣ Google Slashed Its Premium AI Plan by 60% 💸
In a move aimed straight at mass adoption, Google cut its top-tier AI Ultra subscription from $249.99 to $99.99 a month. The company argued AI is now a genuine business utility too — telling enterprises it can save large companies up to $1 billion a year. Cheaper frontier access is how Google plans to win the agent race on volume.

9️⃣ The Full-Stack Flex: Eighth-Generation TPUs ⚙️
Pichai pointed to Google's silicon as its quiet superpower, highlighting eighth-generation TPUs — TPU 8t for training and TPU 8i for inference. The TPU 8i, built with MediaTek, is engineered to deliver inference at 20–30% lower cost, a critical edge as agentic AI multiplies compute demand. "From the infrastructure behind TPU 8i to the frontier capabilities of Gemini 3.5," he said, Google now controls the entire stack.

🔟 Antigravity 2.0: Developers Become Agent Managers 🛠️
Google expanded Antigravity, its agent-first development platform, with Antigravity 2.0 — a standalone desktop app that lets developers orchestrate multiple AI agents working in parallel. It ships with built-in terminal sandboxing, credential masking, and hardened Git policies, alongside a proposed open web standard called WebMCP. The message to developers: your job is shifting from writing every line to directing a team of agents.

⸻

Final Thought

💡 Google I/O 2026 wasn't a list of features — it was a declaration that the assistant era is ending and the agent era has begun. With models that simulate reality, agents that run your life in the background, and a price cut designed to put it all in billions of hands, Pichai is betting Google's entire stack — chips, models, Search, and hardware — on autonomy. Whether or not "agentic" becomes the word of the year, this keynote is the clearest map yet of where the next phase of AI is heading.
