🚀 Sam Altman: Sora's Death, Disney's Heartbreak, Pentagon Regrets & the Coming Superintelligence New Deal — His Most Candid Interview Yet

🎥 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mJSnn0GZmls

In a rare, unguarded sit-down at his home with journalist Laurie Segall on Mostly Human, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman broke his silence on Sora's shutdown, the Pentagon deal backlash, and his radical vision for a post-scarcity AI economy. This is the interview where the mask slips — and what's underneath is more fascinating than you'd expect.

Here's what he actually said:

⸻

Key Takeaways

1️⃣ Sora Was Killed for a Bigger Bet, Not Because It Failed 🎬
"We needed to concentrate our compute and our product capacity into these next generation of automated researchers and companies." Altman revealed that OpenAI was considering folding Sora directly into ChatGPT — but consciously chose not to, fearing it "would have put a series of incentives on us and would've led to a bunch of decisions to win that we just didn't want to make." The world's most hyped video AI was sacrificed so OpenAI could build autonomous AI research labs that could compress years of science into months.

2️⃣ "I Felt Terrible" — The Disney Conversation That Broke His Heart 😔
"The very first thing that the new Disney CEO Josh said to me, and I felt, like, terrible… He's like, 'I get it.' But it's super sad always to disappoint a partner or users or a team." Altman disclosed that he personally had to break the news to Disney's newly installed CEO Josh D'Amaro that Sora was gone — before Disney could launch a single character in the platform. Despite the gut-punch, OpenAI and Disney are still actively in talks, signaling the partnership isn't dead, just radically reset.

3️⃣ The One-Person Billion-Dollar Company Already Exists 💼
"It is a legitimate single person… a massively successful company with one person." Altman confirmed on-air that his long-standing prediction has already come true — someone in his inner circle of tech CEOs is running a massively successful billion-dollar company entirely solo, powered by AI agents. In his CEO group chat, there's been a betting pool for the year this would happen, and Altman says the answer is now.

4️⃣ Pentagon Deal: "We Definitely Rushed It. It Looked Opportunistic and Sloppy." ⚠️
Altman admitted on the podcast that OpenAI "definitely rushed" its February 2026 deal with the Department of War — struck just hours after rival Anthropic was blacklisted from federal systems by the Trump administration. He said the rollout "definitely looked opportunistic and sloppy," acknowledging a rare public failure of judgment. OpenAI subsequently rewrote the agreement to explicitly prohibit domestic mass surveillance and require human accountability for the use of force.

5️⃣ Governments Must Be More Powerful Than AI Companies — Full Stop 🏛️
"One of the most important questions the world will have to answer in the next year is, Are AI companies or are governments more powerful? And I think it's very important that the governments are more powerful." Altman argued that democratically elected institutions — not tech CEOs — must ultimately control how AI is deployed for national security. He admitted he was "miscalibrated" on public distrust, a striking acknowledgement from the man aggressively pushing AI into every sector of society.

6️⃣ AI Superintelligence Is So Transformative It Demands a 'New Deal' 📜
"It is so close, so mind-bending, so disruptive that America needs a new social contract — on the scale of the Progressive Era and the New Deal during the Great Depression." OpenAI simultaneously published a 13-page policy blueprint, "Industrial Policy for the Intelligence Age," proposing a public AI wealth fund giving every American a dividend stake, higher capital gains taxes, a robot tax, and a four-day workweek. Altman says the urgency is real, though critics charge the proposals are designed to preempt tougher regulation.

7️⃣ AI Researchers Will Compress a Decade of Science Into a Single Year 🔬
Altman described a near-term future where networks of automated AI researchers could "compress a decade of scientific discovery into a single year," reshaping medicine, climate, and energy. He said "abundance" is the single word defining his life's work — that humanity has spent millennia learning to manage scarcity, and now must rapidly learn to manage an era of "abundance" it has never faced. The implication: what we've done in centuries of science, AI may achieve in years.

8️⃣ The Jobs Warning Nobody Wants to Hear: "The Next Few Years Are Going to Be Painful" 💸
"If it's hard in many of our current jobs to outwork a GPU, then that changes." While Altman says he's "not a long-term jobs doomer" and believes humanity will invent new roles, he warned that "the next few years are going to be a painful adjustment" filled with "very intense and uncomfortable debates" about remaking society. He went further than most CEOs, admitting that AI is breaking the fundamental labor-capital power balance capitalism has depended on for centuries.

9️⃣ The Parenting Paradox: ChatGPT's Creator Is Delaying AI for His Own Son 👶
"I want him to play in the dirt." The man who couldn't survive new parenthood without ChatGPT — describing it as his indispensable guide through his son's first milestones — is now actively delaying his toddler's AI exposure, saying he'd rather be "on the late end of what's reasonable." In a memorable moment, he turned to host Laurie Segall and told her she "shouldn't let her son use it yet" either, igniting debate about what responsible AI parenting even looks like when the tech's own creator has doubts.

🔟 AI Won't Kill Human Creativity in Hollywood — It Will Make It More Valuable 🎬
"My instinct is it's going to go the other way and people will care more about humans and more about human creators in the future, not less." Directly challenging Hollywood's existential fear that AI will commoditize storytelling, Altman argued that "people really care about other people" — and that AI-generated content will sharpen, not diminish, audiences' appetite for human artistry. With Sora's shutdown leaving Disney studios in limbo, this is both reassurance and a bet on a fundamentally reimagined creative partnership.

Final Thought

💡 This is the most revealing Sam Altman interview in years — not because he controlled the narrative, but because for once he didn't. From Pentagon regrets and a tearful call to Disney to a father who tells parents not to let their kids use the product he built, Altman showed more contradictions, more humanity, and more unguarded candor than any prepared keynote ever could. The man shaping the most consequential technology in history is also genuinely, visibly grappling with what he's unleashed — and that might be the most important thing to know about where we're headed.
